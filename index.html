About

I am Mandar Sharad Bhosale, a passionate Mathematics Educator and Researcher with a Master’s degree in Mathematics. Currently, I am serving as an Assistant Professor at Maharshi Dayanand College, where I teach Quantitative Techniques and conduct programming practicals for B.Sc. students. My teaching journey spans diverse institutions, including St. Xavier’s College and IIT Mumbai, where I have mentored students in subjects like Number Theory, Linear Algebra, and Scientific Communication. My area of research lies in Mathematics Education, with a specific focus on developing combinatorial thinking and integrating technology into mathematics education to enhance learning outcomes. I have collaborated with organizations like iMerit Technologies and Scale AI to design AI-driven content in advanced mathematics. My favorite subjects include Number Theory, Quantitative Techniques, Vedic Mathematics, Combinatorics, and Computer Programming. Beyond academia, I enjoy writing poems in Marathi and Hindi, exploring various cultures, and immersing myself in diverse genres of music. I aspire to create innovative tools for teaching and learning mathematics while inspiring students worldwide to develop a deep appreciation for the subject.
